## Verbatim Surrender Notes

**Moosomin** (UNIQUE_ID: PRF112, surrendered: 1909, dist: 9,009 m)
> Surrendered for sale
> Provided hay lands in 1887 to be shared with #115 Thunderchild. Settlers and government pursued surrender despute continuous rejections by Moosomin band. Total surrender acquired in 1909 of both #112 and #112A. #112B surveyed as a replacement.

**Thunderchild** (UNIQUE_ID: PRF115, surrendered: 1908, dist: 15,068 m)
> Surrendered for sale
> Signed an adhesion to Treaty 6, 1879. Initial survey 1881. 115A added in 1884, 112A Hay Lands added in 1887 (surrendered 640 acers in 1909). Entire reserve surrendered in 1908. Replaced by 115B.

**Thunderchild** (UNIQUE_ID: PRF115A, surrendered: 1908, dist: 15,992 m)
> Surrendered for sale
> Surveyed in 1884, unsure of usage or uptake. No population data outside of total Thunderchild population count. Total surrender 1908.

**Moosomin** (UNIQUE_ID: PRF112A, surrendered: 1909, dist: 19,570 m)
> Surrendered for sale
> Hay land provided to #112 Moosomin and #115 Thunderchild in 1887. 640 acres surrendered 1909, 640 acres retained.

**Grizzly Bear's Head and Lean Man** (UNIQUE_ID: PRF110 & 111-2, surrendered: 1910, dist: 20,482 m)
> Grand Trunk Pacific Railway granted throughway through Order-in-Council.
> Signed adhesion to Treaty 4 in 1877. Reserve surveyed adjacent to #109 Mosquito in 1884. 14,532 acres surrednered for sale in 1905.

**Grizzly Bear's Head/Lean Man** (UNIQUE_ID: PRF110 & 111-1, surrendered: 1905, dist: 20,664 m)
> Surrendered for sale
> Signed adhesion to Treaty 4 in 1877. Reserve surveyed adjacent to #109 Mosquito in 1884. 14,532 acres surrednered for sale in 1905.

**Mosquito** (UNIQUE_ID: PRF109, surrendered: 1910, dist: 23,641 m)
> Grand Trunk Pacific Railway granted throughway by Order-in-Council.
> Signed adhesion to Treaty 6 in 1878. Reserve initially surveyed in 1879, Chief Mosquito chose land adjacent to Red Pheasant #108 in Eagle Hills instead. GTR pursued surrender for "right of way".

**Little Pine** (UNIQUE_ID: PRF116, surrendered: 1927, dist: 49,596 m)
> Patented to the HBC sometime after the initial survey in 1887, returned to the department in 1927.
> Signed Treaty 6 adhesion, 1879. Among others near Fort Walsh in early 1880s. Some of Lucky Man's followers were incorporated into Battleford bands. Two sections patented to HBC in reserve, later returned. Seemingly, no other surrenders.
