## Verbatim Surrender Notes

**Cote** (UNIQUE_ID: PRQA64, surrendered: 1906, dist: 0 m)
> Surrendered for sale and settlement of Kamsack
> Initial survey 1877, 35 families. CNR pursued surrender in 1904, 1905, and 1907 for settlement of Kamsack and settler farming in the area. More sections surrendered in 1913, no immediate buyers, land returned by 1916/17.

**The Key** (UNIQUE_ID: PRQA65, surrendered: 1909, dist: 18,575 m)
> Surrendered for sale
> Initial survey set out in 1876 near Swan River, settled there for some time. Moved to Fort Pelly area in 1882. New survey in 1884 at 38 sq. miles. 11,500 acres surrendered for sale in 1909.

**Keeseekoose** (UNIQUE_ID: PRQA66, surrendered: 1909, dist: 21,205 m)
> Surrendered to be sold in 1909, $85 paid to each individual.
> initial survey 1884. Confusing extensions made in the 1890s, seemingly the 1884 survey stuck. In 1909, 7600 acres "unanimously" surrendered to be sold.
